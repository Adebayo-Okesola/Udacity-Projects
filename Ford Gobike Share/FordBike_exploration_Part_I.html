What is the structure of your dataset?¶

The data set consist of 183412 obsrvations (rows) and 16 variables(column). It consist of various type of variables such as;

Norminal variables (end_station_name, start_station_name, end_station_id, start_station_id, and bike_id)

Categorical variables (member_gender, user_type, and bike_share_for_all_trip)

Numerical variables (start_station_latitude, start_station_longitude, end_station_latitude, end_station_longitude, member_birth_year, duration_sec, start time and end time).

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

The main features of interest in this dataset are;

1.) the gender distribution of those involved in the bike share

2.) The Age distribution of riders it relation to distance and duration

3.) Time taken for member to reach the end satation base on distance

4.) The user type involvement in relation to age, distance and duration

5.) The Distance between stations

6.) user type uses the bike share for all their trips

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

Features in the dataset that will help support this investigation are the following columns; Duration, start station latitude and longitude, end station latitude and longitude, member_gender,user_type, member_birth_year, bike_share_for_all_trip.

# CALCULATION FOR DISTANCE USING LONG AND LATITUDE "calc_dict" function
# NOTE: Code gotten from https://stackoverflow.com/questions/40452759/
def calc_dist(lat1, long1, lat2, long2, to_radians=True, earth_radius=6371):
    """
    slightly modified version: of http://stackoverflow.com/a/29546836/2901002

    Calculate the great circle distance between two points
    on the earth (specified in decimal degrees or in radians)

    All (lat, long) coordinates must have numeric dtypes and be of equal length.

    """
    if to_radians:
        lat1, long1, lat2, long2 = np.radians([lat1, long1, lat2, long2])

    a = np.sin((lat2-lat1)/2.0)**2 + \
        np.cos(lat1) * np.cos(lat2) * np.sin((long2-long1)/2.0)**2
    
    # multiply by Earth radius to get kilometers
    return earth_radius * 2 * np.arcsin(np.sqrt(a))


# calculate the distance per trip
Bike_clean['distance'] = calc_dist(Bike_clean.start_station_latitude, Bike_clean.start_station_longitude,
                    Bike_clean.end_station_latitude, Bike_clean.end_station_longitude)

Conclusions¶

Having carried out an exploratory anaysis of the Ford GoBike data set, which includes information about individuals who made rides in a bike-sharing system covering the greater San Francisco Bay area in February 2019. It was observed that majority of the riders are men from the 18-40years followed 41-60years and most of them were subscribers.

Most people did not use the bike share for all their trip, which shows that more people cover distance of 10 km with duration of 5 hours. Looking at the relationship between the distance, duration and age, it was observed that majority of those who use the bike share the most are the youth(20 - 40) categotry. With respect to distance and duration, there is a weak positive correlation that is an increase in distance result in an increase in duration.

The youth and subscribers have more tendency to embark on long-distance rides although on average all age group travels almost the same distance though the tendency to travel more distance decreases as age increases.

Limitation¶

The birth year column with missing data were filled with 2019 which is the year the data was collected.

The member gender column was with missing value was replaced with "undisclosed" this is because the affected roles contain some useful data for the analysis.

The missing values in the start station name and end station were not dropped but was left just to retain some vital data.

During visualization some rows were drop along the line to have a good plotting scale.
